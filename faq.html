FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

1. How much do Indian wedding invitation cards typically cost?
The cost of invitation cards usually depends upon the number of cards, the design details, finishing options (foiling, letterpress, embossing), customization, and printing style. Wedding invitation cards start from as low as INR 20 and can go up depending on your selection.
2. Do you provide samples before bulk printing?
Yes, we can provide you with sample cards before bulk printing, so you can check the final look and quality. The sample process helps avoid mistakes during the final print.
3. Do you provide printing services?
Yes, we provide both card designing and complete printing services. We can do personalized printing with your wedding text, design elements, and logos.
4. Can customers send their text for the complete procedure or content?
Customers can send us the wedding texts, procedures, and special wordings they want to include. Based on your needs, we design and print the content on your selected card design.
5. Can we order add-ons to match the invitation?
Yes, we provide a large range of add-ons like: Menu card, RSVP card, Thank you card, Welcome card, Reception card, and more – all coordinated to match the main wedding card design.
6. Can we suggest our own card size and design?
Yes you can select appropriate sizes for your card with related logos, fonts, texts, and any type of customized design.
7. Do the prices include taxes and shipping cost?
Taxes & import duties (for overseas) are not included. Shipping charges will be extra. We ensure cost-effective shipping by choosing trusted courier partners and providing accurate delivery times.
8. If we want more inserts to our card, will the charges be more?
Yes, if you want extra inserts for your card, the charges for the inserts and extra printing will be added accordingly.
9. How much time is required for printing and shipping?
After your order approval, we need around 10-15 business days for production. Delivery time will depend on your location. Please plan in advance to avoid last-minute delays.
